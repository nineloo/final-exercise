Black Bieber, 2014

If you were ever wondering how it feels to see your culture commercialized, this GIF tells it all. Created by RaFia Santana under their tag name “RaFia” in 2014, Santana combines a color whirlwind with the face of a youthful Justin Bieber. The visuals of Santana’s image dance around the magazine category with a magenta headline “Enforcer” to drive the message along. Black Bieber addresses how standards in black culture are too often up for grabs. A flattened bright pink durag (a staple in the black haircare world) is dawned on Justin Bieber's head, with a duo of shiny cuban linked chains accessorizing a harmful trope. This image not only registers Santana’s frustration with pop culture using ‘blackness’ to push the opposite faces ahead. It displays the façade in action; especially when a gradient flash switches Bieber’s white skin to a chestnut brown.

#PAYBLACKTIME, 2016 - present

This ongoing project that began abruptly after the 2016 presidential election of the United States addresses how art is not only a political tool. It can become an act of support for people left our of the conversation. #PAYBLACKTIME opperated as a money transferring service that requested financial donations from White Americans. In return, those donations would be carried over into a fund to provide meals for black and brown communities. Santana’s incredibly unique online project arrived when white supremacy in America was resurrecting to a horrifying degree. It provided space for people of color to uproot the platforms in favor of the presidential administration like Facebook and PayPal.
